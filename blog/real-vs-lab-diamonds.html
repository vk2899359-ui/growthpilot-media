Real vs Lab-Grown Diamonds: Everything You Need to Know

As lab-grown diamonds gain popularity in Gurgaon and across India, buyers face a new question: natural or lab-created? This guide from Auric Jewels' expert gemologists breaks down the differences to help you make the right choice.

What Are Natural (Real) Diamonds?

Natural diamonds are formed deep within the Earth's mantle under extreme heat (around 2,200°F) and pressure over 1 to 3 billion years. They are brought to the surface through volcanic eruptions and mined from kimberlite pipes. Each natural diamond is a unique geological miracle — no two are identical in their inclusion patterns, making every stone a one-of-a-kind creation of nature.

Natural diamonds have been treasured for centuries as symbols of love, status, and permanence. They are the traditional choice for engagement rings, heirloom pieces, and investment jewellery. What Are Lab-Grown Diamonds?

Lab-grown diamonds (also called lab-created, synthetic, or cultured diamonds) are produced in controlled laboratory environments using two primary methods:

HPHT (High Pressure High Temperature): Replicates the natural diamond formation process using a diamond seed, carbon source, and extreme pressure and heat. Produces diamonds in weeks rather than billions of years.

CVD (Chemical Vapor Deposition): Uses a thin diamond seed placed in a sealed chamber filled with carbon-rich gas. The gas is heated until carbon atoms attach to the seed, growing the diamond layer by layer. CVD is the more common method for jewellery-quality diamonds.

Lab-grown diamonds are chemically, physically, and optically identical to natural diamonds. They have the same carbon crystal structure, the same hardness (10 on the Mohs scale), and the same refractive index. Even expert gemologists cannot distinguish them without specialised equipment.

Pros and Cons of Natural Diamonds

Pros

  • Rarity and romance: Formed over billions of years, each stone is a unique natural wonder
  • Resale value: Natural diamonds hold and can appreciate in value, especially certified stones above 1ct
  • Heirloom quality: Can be passed down generations with increasing sentimental and financial value
  • Universally recognised: The traditional and universally accepted choice for engagements worldwide
  • Investment grade: Rare, high-quality natural diamonds (D-F, IF-VVS, 2ct+) have historically appreciated

Cons

  • Higher price: Natural diamonds cost significantly more than equivalent lab-grown stones
  • Environmental concerns: Diamond mining has an environmental footprint, though the industry is improving
  • Ethical considerations: While the Kimberley Process has reduced conflict diamonds, concerns persist

Pros and Cons of Lab-Grown Diamonds

Pros

  • 60-70% lower price: Get a larger or higher-quality diamond for the same budget
  • Identical appearance: Visually and chemically indistinguishable from natural diamonds
  • No mining: No land disruption or mining-related environmental impact
  • Guaranteed origin: Complete traceability from lab to jeweller
  • Larger stones accessible: A 2ct lab-grown diamond costs what a 0.80ct natural might cost

Cons

  • Limited resale value: The resale market for lab-grown diamonds is still developing and prices are declining
  • Not rare: Can be produced in unlimited quantities, lacking the uniqueness of natural stones
  • Declining prices: As technology improves and production scales, prices continue to fall
  • Perception: Some buyers and cultures still consider only natural diamonds "real"

Which Should You Choose?

The right choice depends entirely on your priorities, values, and intended use:

Choose Natural Diamonds if:
  • You value rarity, tradition, and the romance of a billion-year-old stone
  • You're buying for an engagement, heirloom, or investment
  • Resale value and long-term appreciation matter to you
  • You want a piece that holds deep symbolic significance
Choose Lab-Grown Diamonds if:
  • You want maximum size and quality within your budget
  • Environmental and ethical sourcing is a top priority
  • You're buying fashion jewellery or pieces for regular rotation
  • You're comfortable with the stone having limited resale value

Both natural and lab-grown diamonds are real diamonds in every chemical and physical sense. The choice between them isn't about quality — it's about what matters most to you: the romance of rarity, the practicality of value, the ethics of sourcing, or the long-term financial picture. There's no wrong answer.
